Irony, Sweet Irony
They said one thing. The universe did the opposite. We call this comedy.
STEVE IRWIN1962 – 2006
I have no fear of losing my life — if I have to save a koala or a crocodile, I will do it.
☠
CAUSE OF DEATH
Pierced through the heart by a stingray barb while snorkelling on the Great Barrier Reef. Not a crocodile. Not a koala. A stingray.
AESCHYLUS525 BC – 456 BC
God lends a helping hand to the man who tries hard.
☠
CAUSE OF DEATH
An eagle, seeking a rock to crack open a tortoise, mistook his bald head for one. Dropped it on him from altitude. God's aim, apparently, was off.
FRANCIS BACON1561 – 1626
Knowledge is power.
☠
CAUSE OF DEATH
Died testing his theory that snow could preserve meat, stuffing a chicken with it in freezing temperatures. Pneumonia followed. The chicken survived. Bacon did not.
TYCHO BRAHE1546 – 1601
It would be shameful if, having lived for so great a purpose, I were to die for so small a one.
☠
CAUSE OF DEATH
Refused to leave a royal banquet to relieve himself — considered it rude. His bladder either burst or became fatally infected. He died eleven days later. The etiquette held. He didn't.
TENNESSEE WILLIAMS1911 – 1983
I don't want realism. I want magic.
☠
CAUSE OF DEATH
Choked to death on a bottle cap in his New York hotel room. He apparently used his mouth to open bottles. The most realistic death imaginable. No magic.
ALLAN PINKERTON1819 – 1884
We Never Sleep.(Pinkerton National Detective Agency motto)
☠
CAUSE OF DEATH
Bit his own tongue while walking and developed gangrene from the wound. The man who never slept died because he couldn't watch where he was going.
DRACO650 BC – 600 BC
Most crimes deserve death. I cannot find a harsher penalty for worse ones.
☠
CAUSE OF DEATH
Smothered to death by cloaks. His admirers at the theatre, expressing love in the Athenian custom of throwing hats and garments at those they admired, buried him alive in fabric. The harshest lawgiver in history was killed by appreciation.
HARRY HOUDINI1874 – 1926
My brain is the key that sets me free.
☠
CAUSE OF DEATH
A student asked if he could really take any punch to the stomach. Houdini had not tensed his muscles before the blows landed. He died nine days later of peritonitis from a ruptured appendix. The world's greatest escape artist could not escape his own abdomen.
MARK TWAIN1835 – 1910
I came in with Halley's Comet in 1835. It is coming again next year, and I expect to go out with it.
☠
CAUSE OF DEATH
Heart attack on April 21, 1910 — one day after Halley's Comet made its closest pass to Earth. He said it in 1909, when no one expected him to die. He was right to the day. Even his death was good writing.
JAMES DEAN1931 – 1955
Take it easy driving — the life you save may be mine.
☠
CAUSE OF DEATH
Killed in a high-speed collision in his Porsche Spyder on Route 466 in California, weeks after filming a highway safety PSA with exactly those words. He was 24. He was on his way to a race.
ATTILA THE HUN406 – 453
Trample the weak. Hurdle the dead.
☠
CAUSE OF DEATH
The man who terrorised two Roman empires and razed half of Europe choked to death on his own blood at his wedding feast. He got a nosebleed, passed out drunk, and drowned in it. No battlefield. No enemy. Just wine, a bride, and poor sleeping position.
WILLIAM HENRY HARRISON1773 – 1841
The strongest of all governments is that which is most free.
☠
CAUSE OF DEATH
Delivered the longest inaugural address in US presidential history — 8,445 words, hatless, coatless, in freezing rain, for nearly two hours — to prove he was too vigorous to be dismissed as old. Died of pneumonia 31 days later. Shortest presidency in American history. The speech outlived the man by centuries.
CHRYSIPPUS279 BC – 206 BC
The wise man does nothing he has cause to regret.
☠
CAUSE OF DEATH
The Stoic philosopher watched his donkey eat figs, told his servant to give it wine to wash them down, found this so funny that he died laughing. The founder of systematic logic and one of antiquity's most prolific thinkers was undone by a donkey having a drink. He reportedly had no regrets.
CLEMENT VALLANDIGHAM1820 – 1871
The facts will speak for themselves. I am here to prove them.
☠
CAUSE OF DEATH
A defense attorney arguing that his client couldn't have shot himself, he decided to demonstrate. In his hotel room, he picked up a pistol he believed to be unloaded and re-enacted the scene. It was loaded. He shot himself through the head. His client was acquitted.

Because the Dead Can't Be Mad at Me
Real quotes. Lightly adjusted. Their estates may send letters.
MARIE CURIE1867 – 1934
Nothing in life is to be feared, only to be understood.Nothing in life is to be feared — certainly not a little radiation. Carry on.
☠
CAUSE OF DEATH
Aplastic anaemia from decades of radiation exposure. Her notebooks are still too radioactive to handle safely. They're kept in lead-lined boxes in Paris.
ELVIS PRESLEY1935 – 1977
Ambition is a dream with a V8 engine.Ambition is a dream with a V8 engine, a fully stocked medicine cabinet, and a reinforced toilet seat.
☠
CAUSE OF DEATH
Found unresponsive on his bathroom floor at Graceland. Cardiac arrhythmia, significantly linked to prescription drug use. The King left the building.
SIGMUND FREUD1856 – 1939
Dreams are the royal road to the unconscious.Dreams are the royal road to the unconscious. Cigars are the royal road to jaw cancer. I have tried both extensively.
☠
CAUSE OF DEATH
Oral cancer after smoking up to 20 cigars a day for most of his adult life. Had 30+ surgeries on his jaw. When asked if a cigar was a phallic symbol, he reportedly said "sometimes a cigar is just a cigar." Not this time.
ISADORA DUNCAN1877 – 1927
You were once wild here. Don't let them tame you.You were once wild here. Don't let them tame you. Do, however, tuck in your scarf before getting into a convertible.
☠
CAUSE OF DEATH
Her long silk scarf tangled in the rear wheel of a Bugatti Type 35, breaking her neck instantly. Her last words were reportedly "Farewell, my friends, I go to glory."
OSCAR WILDE1854 – 1900
I can resist anything except temptation.I can resist anything except temptation. The wallpaper in this room, however, is putting up a fair fight.
☠
CAUSE OF DEATH
Cerebral meningitis, dying in a dingy Paris hotel. He reportedly said "My wallpaper and I are fighting a duel to the death. One or other of us has to go." The wallpaper won.
NAPOLEON BONAPARTE1769 – 1821
Impossible is a word found only in the dictionary of fools.Impossible is a word found only in the dictionary of fools. "Exile" and "island" are found in everyone's.
☠
CAUSE OF DEATH
Died in exile on Saint Helena, a remote island in the South Atlantic. Stomach cancer — or possibly arsenic poisoning from his green wallpaper. Conquered Europe. Could not conquer an island.
EDGAR ALLAN POE1809 – 1849
The boundaries which divide life from death are at best shadowy and vague.The boundaries which divide life from death are at best shadowy and vague. This may explain why I was found delirious in someone else's clothes outside a polling station in Baltimore.
☠
CAUSE OF DEATH
Found semi-conscious in a gutter in Baltimore, wearing clothes not his own. Died four days later raving in a hospital. Cause unknown — leading theories include rabies, cooping (a voter fraud scheme), or cholera. Even his death reads like one of his stories.
VOLTAIRE1694 – 1778
God is a comedian playing to an audience that is too afraid to laugh.God is a comedian playing to an audience too afraid to laugh. On reflection, I may have been too specific about the bit where I die.
☠
CAUSE OF DEATH
Returned to Paris in triumph at 83 after 28 years in exile, attended six standing ovations, and was crowned with a laurel wreath at the theatre. The excitement proved fatal. He was dead within weeks. Even his death was a performance — just a final one.
JACK DANIEL1850 – 1911
Every day we make it, we make it the best we can.Every day we make it, we make it the best we can. Some days are harder than others. Especially when you've kicked the safe.
☠
CAUSE OF DEATH
Kicked his office safe in frustration after forgetting the combination. The resulting injury to his toe became infected, spread through his body, and killed him six years later. The man behind the world's most famous whiskey was brought down not by drink, but by arithmetic.
MARCUS AURELIUS121 AD – 180 AD
You have power over your mind, not outside events. Realize this and you will find strength.You have power over your mind, not outside events. Realize this and you will find strength. The plague, however, is outside events.
☠
CAUSE OF DEATH
Died of the Antonine Plague — the same pandemic he'd spent much of his reign managing, writing stoically about the acceptance of death, and watching kill five million of his subjects. He had ample time to make peace with it. He apparently did.
PLINY THE ELDER23 AD – 79 AD
The only certainty is that nothing is certain.The only certainty is that nothing is certain. Except Vesuvius. Vesuvius was quite certain.
☠
CAUSE OF DEATH
Sailed toward Vesuvius during its eruption — to investigate it. He was the most prolific natural historian of the ancient world and could not resist a closer look. He died on the beach at Stabiae, likely from toxic fumes. Curiosity killed the naturalist.
LEON TROTSKY1879 – 1940
The end may justify the means as long as there is something that justifies the end.The end may justify the means as long as there is something that justifies the end. An ice axe to the skull, it turns out, is quite a definitive end.
☠
CAUSE OF DEATH
Assassinated in his Mexican exile by a Stalinist agent who drove an ice axe into his skull. He had spent his life theorising about revolutionary violence as a political tool. Stalin, a keen student, had simply applied the lesson. Trotsky survived the initial blow long enough to fight his attacker. He died the next day.
RASPUTIN1869 – 1916
Love life and it will love you back.Love life and it will love you back. Though in my experience, some noblemen will try very hard to change this arrangement.
☠
CAUSE OF DEATH
Poisoned, shot twice, beaten, and thrown into a frozen river by Russian aristocrats who were alarmed by his influence over the Tsar. Each method was expected to be sufficient. None were enough on their own. He drowned in the end. The cause of death on his autopsy: drowning. History found this unsatisfying and added the rest anyway.
VINCENT VAN GOGH1853 – 1890
I dream of painting and then I paint my dream.I dream of painting and then I paint my dream. The ear was a miscalculation. The rest I stand by.
☠
CAUSE OF DEATH
Died of a gunshot wound in a wheat field in Auvers-sur-Oise at 37. He sold one painting in his lifetime. Within a decade of his death, his work was worth fortunes. He never knew. His brother Theo, who had funded his entire career, died six months later. The dream was painted. Nobody was watching.

JIM FIXX1932 – 1984
Running has given me a glimpse of the greatest freedom a man can ever know.
☠
CAUSE OF DEATH
Died of a massive heart attack mid-run on a Vermont road. The man who wrote The Complete Book of Running and popularised jogging in America died doing exactly that.
POETIC
HUNTER S. THOMPSON1937 – 2005
Buy the ticket, take the ride.
☠
CAUSE OF DEATH
Died by self-inflicted gunshot at his compound in Colorado, at 67. He had written: "I would feel real trapped in this life if I didn't know I could commit suicide at any time." He bought the ticket. He took the ride.
POETIC
ANDRÉ THE GIANT1946 – 1993
I'm not a giant. I'm just very, very large.
☠
CAUSE OF DEATH
Congestive heart failure — a direct complication of acromegaly, the condition that made him 7'4" and over 500 lbs. His heart, like the rest of him, simply grew too large for the world.
POETIC
HEATH LEDGER1979 – 2008
I don't have a technique. I've never been able to repeat anything I've done. I'm always searching.
☠
CAUSE OF DEATH
Accidental prescription drug overdose. He had thrown himself entirely into the Joker — sleeping two hours a night, keeping a diary of the character's psychology. He never stopped searching. He never stopped.
POETIC
KARL MARX1818 – 1883
The philosophers have only interpreted the world. The point is to change it.
☠
CAUSE OF DEATH
Pleurisy and bronchitis, dying in poverty in London, stateless. His ideas went on to reshape entire continents, topple empires, and kill millions. He changed the world. He just didn't live to see it — and probably wouldn't have recognised it.
POETIC
ALEXANDER THE GREAT356 BC – 323 BC
There is nothing impossible to him who will try.
☠
CAUSE OF DEATH
Died in Babylon at 32, after conquering most of the known world. Cause unknown — typhoid, poisoning, excessive drinking, or grief after Hephaestion's death. He tried everything. Even dying young, he made it legendary.
POETIC
AYRTON SENNA1960 – 1994
If you no longer go for a gap that exists, you are no longer a racing driver.
☠
CAUSE OF DEATH
Died at the San Marino Grand Prix at Imola when his Williams hit the concrete wall at Tamburello corner at 190 mph. He had won three world championships. He had always gone for the gap. That day, the gap wasn't there.
POETIC
GENERAL JOHN SEDGWICK1813 – 1864
They couldn't hit an elephant at this dist—
☠
CAUSE OF DEATH
Shot through the head by a Confederate sniper at the Battle of Spotsylvania Court House, mid-sentence. He had been mocking his troops for ducking enemy fire. These were his final, unfinished words. History completed the quote for him.
POETIC
STEVE JOBS1955 – 2011
Believing that the dots will connect down the road will give you the confidence to follow your heart.
☠
CAUSE OF DEATH
Pancreatic cancer, after reportedly delaying surgery for nine months in favour of alternative treatments — fruit diets, acupuncture, herbal remedies. The man who trusted his instincts above all others trusted them here too. The dots did not connect in time.
POETIC
DENNIS WILSON1944 – 1983
The ocean is my only friend.
☠
CAUSE OF DEATH
The only actual surfer in the Beach Boys drowned in Marina Del Rey, diving drunk off a friend's boat trying to retrieve belongings he'd thrown overboard years earlier. He was buried at sea by special presidential waiver. His only friend kept him.
POETIC
KURT COBAIN1967 – 1994
It's better to burn out than to fade away.
☠
CAUSE OF DEATH
He left this Neil Young line in his suicide note. He was 27. Nirvana had sold 75 million records. He burned. He did not fade. Whether that was the point or the tragedy depends entirely on which generation you ask.
POETIC
JEAN-BAPTISTE LULLY1632 – 1687
Music is the arithmetic of sounds as optics is the geometry of light.
☠
CAUSE OF DEATH
The Baroque composer was conducting a performance for Louis XIV using a long staff to beat time on the floor — the custom before the baton — when he drove it through his own foot. Gangrene set in. Doctors recommended amputation. He refused, unwilling to stop performing. The music went on. He did not.
POETIC
GEORGE GORDON BYRON1788 – 1824
I only go out too much to get out of the way when people are talking nonsense.
☠
CAUSE OF DEATH
Died in Greece at 36, where he had gone to fight for Greek independence — the most romantic war available. He never saw battle. He caught a fever riding in the rain, and his doctors bled him repeatedly despite his protests. He died of the treatment, not the illness. The most dramatic poet in England got a very undramatic death.
POETIC
SIGURD THE MIGHTY? – 892
Victory is its own reward.
☠
CAUSE OF DEATH
The Viking Earl of Orkney defeated his enemy Mael Brigte in battle, beheaded him, and tied the head to his saddle as a trophy. Riding home in triumph, the dead man's protruding tooth grazed Sigurd's leg. The wound became infected. He died shortly after. He was killed by a man he had already killed. The head had the last word.
